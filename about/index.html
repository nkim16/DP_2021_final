"Down the Rabbit Hole" is a project about the self and the processes and the tools that are involved in searching: the loss of control and the loss of thoughts that are often involved in browsing. By looking back at my search history, I realize how dependent I am on these platforms and how our actions are constantly directed by tools--tools that are given to us by these big corporations. By looking back at my search history, I realize this tool stands for my than how I view myself but how these corporations view me. I wanted to attempt to reveal this human and intimate process of how an excess of information often leads us to go down a rabbit hole through our interactions and the trail that is left behind in our search history that is then picked up by Google and used to promote our rabbit hole tendencies.

But how true is this identity that I present on the internet? Is it an overly simplified version of who I truely am? By going down a rabbit hole, I am navigating websites that were never intended to be saved it's an archive of something that seemed useless and mindless that turned into somethig permanent. But these footprints are the only thing left behind and is our identity once we shut off our computers. This website which essentially stands as a self portrait; is about my searching habits and the loss of control that is often felt with entering these rabbit holes.
